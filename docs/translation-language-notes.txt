Mr Mallorca Golf Translation Language Notes

Use the master prompt first.
Then add the relevant language note below before the English source.

Spanish
- Write for a golfer in Spain, not a hotel guest.
- Avoid stiff literal phrasing copied from English.
- Prefer natural golf/travel phrasing over corporate language.
- Keep the tone polished but not showy.
- Avoid tourist-board exaggeration.
- Sound like someone who knows golf and the island.

German
- Keep the language clear, controlled, and confident.
- Avoid over-formality and avoid clunky literal sentence structure.
- Golf terms should sound native and credible, not translated from English.
- Strong, practical wording is better than decorative wording.

French
- Avoid glossy brochure language.
- Keep the tone refined but grounded.
- If a sentence sounds too promotional, make it calmer and more credible.
- Golf terminology should feel natural to a golfer, not a tourism editor.

Dutch
- Keep it natural, compact, and understated.
- Avoid sounding translated from English.
- Do not make the tone too formal or too casual.
- Practical clarity matters more than stylistic flourish.

Swedish
- Aim for calm, natural, and confident.
- Avoid sounding over-written or overly translated.
- Keep sentences flowing cleanly without becoming flat or robotic.
- Golf vocabulary should feel normal to an actual player.

Chinese
- Write smooth, natural Mandarin that feels premium and human.
- Avoid robotic sentence structure and direct word-for-word translation.
- Keep golf terminology consistent and easy to read.
- Do not make it too formal, too salesy, or too internet-casual.
- The tone should feel expert, clear, and trustworthy.

Second-Pass Review Prompt

Use this after the first translation draft.

Review this [TARGET LANGUAGE] translation for a premium golf website.

Goal:
Make it sound like it was originally written in [TARGET LANGUAGE] by someone who understands golf and Mallorca.

Check for:
- awkward literal translation
- unnatural golf vocabulary
- wrong tone
- tourism-board language
- AI-sounding phrasing
- punctuation, currency, date, or temperature formatting issues
- grammar mistakes
- wording a local golfer would find tacky or embarrassing

Rules:
- preserve meaning
- preserve structure
- preserve facts
- improve only the wording and naturalness

Return:
1. the improved final translation
2. a short list of any phrases you substantially rewrote and why
