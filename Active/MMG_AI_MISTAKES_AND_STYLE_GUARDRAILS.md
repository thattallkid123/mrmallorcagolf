# MMG AI Mistakes And Style Guardrails

Use this file all the time for MMG writing.
If output conflicts with this file, rewrite it.

## Non-Negotiables

- No em dashes.
- No generic AI openings.
- No travel-brochure filler.
- No fake authority language.
- No inflated luxury wording.
- No "not X, but Y" framing.
- No "here's the truth" or "what people don't realise" hooks.
- No empty three-part fragment lines.

## Punctuation And Rhythm

- Prefer full sentences over chopped fragments.
- Use commas and full stops naturally.
- Use colons sparingly.
- Exclamation marks should be rare.
- Parentheses only when they sound natural in Andy's voice.

## Words And Phrases To Avoid

- stunning
- breathtaking
- nestled
- seamless
- elevate
- world-class as empty praise
- luxury as filler
- unforgettable
- hidden gem
- curated experience
- bespoke as an adjective
- vibrant
- bustling
- exceptional

## AI Patterns To Cut

- "The best part?"
- "Here's the truth"
- "It's not X, it's Y"
- "More than just"
- "Whether you're..."
- "From X to Y"
- "If you're looking for..."
- "In the heart of..."
- "There is something for everyone"

## MMG Voice Check

The writing should feel:

- specific
- calm
- direct
- observed
- useful
- honest

It should not feel:

- salesy
- inflated
- over-polished
- generic
- like a tourism board
- like LinkedIn AI copy

## Andy-Specific Rules

- Write like a PGA professional with judgement.
- Use first-person only when Andy has genuinely played the course.
- Honest negatives improve credibility.
- Practical decisions matter more than adjectives.
- One sharp real observation beats three fancy sentences.

## Final Self-Check

Before finishing, check:

1. Remove em dashes.
2. Cut filler adjectives.
3. Replace vague claims with specifics.
4. Remove obvious AI phrases.
5. Make it sound more like Andy and less like a content writer.
