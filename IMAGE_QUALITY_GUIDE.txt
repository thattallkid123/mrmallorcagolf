PHOTOGRAPHY RECOMMENDATIONS FOR YOUR SHOOT
================================================================================

WHAT TO SHOOT:
1. Golden hour on courses (early morning or late afternoon)
   - Warm, soft lighting
   - Long shadows add dimension
   - Greens look rich and vibrant

2. Course features:
   - Elevated tees with dramatic views
   - Water hazards and bunker complexes
   - Signature holes
   - From angles that show why they matter

3. Wide shots showing:
   - Terrain variation
   - Surrounding landscape
   - Sky (blue, not blown out)

4. Detail shots:
   - Greens and condition
   - Fairways and texture
   - Course markers/signage

5. Lifestyle/personality:
   - You coaching/playing on courses
   - Before/after coaching moments
   - Authentic action shots
   - Client testimonial candids (if applicable)

COLOR GRADE TARGETS:
- Warm tone: Slightly add warmth to match luxury/premium feel
- Saturated greens: Course grass should look rich and healthy
- Clear skies: Aim for 60-70% blue sky in composition
- Sunlight: Golden hour 3500-4500K color temperature
- Consistency: All images should have similar color profile

COMPOSITION STANDARDS:
- Rule of thirds: Key elements at intersections
- Depth: Foreground, midground, background layers
- Elevation: Shoot from tees looking down/across
- Weather: Clear days preferred, dramatic clouds acceptable
- Time: Avoid harsh midday shadows (10am-2pm)
